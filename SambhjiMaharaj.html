SAMBHAJI MAHARAJ

The Lion of Maharashtra | Protector of Dharma

The Valiant Warrior King

Sambhaji Maharaj, the son of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, was a fearless warrior who defended the Maratha Empire against the mighty Mughal forces. His strategic brilliance and unwavering courage made him one of the most formidable rulers of his time.

Life and Legacy

1657: Birth of a Legend

Sambhaji was born on May 14, 1657, at Purandar Fort to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Saibai.

1678: Coronation

Sambhaji was crowned as the second Chhatrapati of the Maratha Empire at Raigad Fort, continuing his father's legacy.

1681-89: Defending the Empire

Sambhaji led numerous successful campaigns against the Mughals, Portuguese, and English, expanding the Maratha dominion.

1689: Supreme Sacrifice

After being captured by Aurangzeb, Sambhaji endured brutal torture but refused to convert or surrender, becoming a symbol of courage and sacrifice.

The Warrior's Code

Courage

"Fear is the only enemy. A true warrior faces challenges head-on with unwavering courage."

Dharma

"Righteousness is the foundation of rule. A king must protect dharma before protecting his kingdom."

Sacrifice

"The greatest honor is to give one's life in defense of one's people and principles."

श्रीमंत योगी

"The Divine Warrior King"

Sambhaji Maharaj's life was a testament to courage, resilience, and devotion to one's principles. His sacrifice became the foundation for the Maratha Empire's later expansion under Chhatrapati Rajaram and Chhatrapati Shahu.
